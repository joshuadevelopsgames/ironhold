## The 24 patterns

### Roof patterns

**P1 — Pitched gable roof with 45° stair-block slope.**
*Universal across all houses.* Long ridge with stairs descending on both
sides at 45°. Gable ends are triangular wall fills. NEVER stepped pyramid
for houses.

**P2 — Conical/pointed-pyramid tower cap.**
*Seen on:* r04, r07, r10, r14L, r15, r17, r22, r23.
Stairs blocks face inward each layer, narrowing toward apex. Often 5 levels
tall over a 5×5 or 7×7 base. Reads as fairy-tale / fantasy.

**P3 — Multi-tier stepped roof with moss/leaf weathering.**
*Seen on:* tavern (Abfielder), r00.
Multiple roof tiers stack at different heights, creating tall narrow
silhouettes. Moss-carpet or azalea-leaves dot the ridges and gable edges
for weathered look.

**P4 — Step-gable / Flemish-gable ends.**
*Seen on:* r02, r07, r12.
Gable end isn't a smooth triangle — it has "step" or scroll-shape edges,
stair-blocks stepping outward at intervals. Reads as Dutch / Flemish /
old urban European.

**P5 — Mixed roof material strips.**
*Seen on:* r07 (dramatically), r08.
Different sections of roof use different materials: dark wood stairs,
red brick stairs, deepslate slabs. The transitions are clean (not random).

**P6 — Curved organic roof transitions.**
*Seen on:* r17.
Where two roof pitches meet at a corner, the transition uses an extra
stair-block to soften the angle. Looks "built by hand" not "extruded."

**P7 — Dormer windows protruding from roof slope.**
*Seen on:* r00, r02, r08, r15, r22.
Small protruding gables in the middle of the long roof slope, each with
its own tiny pitched roof and a window/glass in front. Tells the player
"there's an attic room here."

### Wall / facade patterns

**P8 — Half-timbered (Fachwerk) walls with vertical-only beams.**
*Seen on:* most houses.
Dark vertical oak/dark-oak logs every 2-3 cells, lighter plank infill.

**P9 — Half-timber + diagonal cross-braces.**
*Seen on:* tavern (Abfielder), r00, r16, r17.
Variant of P8 with diagonal stair-blocks crossing selected panels for
extra visual rhythm.

**P10 — Stone base, wood upper.**
*Universal vernacular pattern.* Bottom 1-3 courses always stone (cobble,
stone bricks, deepslate), wood/half-timber above.

**P11 — Bay windows protruding from second floor.**
*Seen on:* r00, r02 (multiple), r07 (many), r17, r18.
Window unit sticks out 1 block from the wall plane, supported below by
a stair-block corbel angled outward. Forms a "lookout perch."

**P12 — Cantilevered upper floor (jetty).**
*Seen on:* medieval-house Abfielder, tavern Abfielder.
The whole upper floor extends 1 block beyond the ground floor. Adds a
strong horizontal shadow line.

**P13 — Diamond-pattern lattice windows.**
*Seen on:* r00, r02, r17, r18, r22.
Window frame contains stair-block diagonals forming a diamond grid pattern.
Suggests leaded-glass.

**P14 — Stained / tinted glass windows.**
*Seen on:* r05, r07, r18 (subtle white-tinted), r17 (white-pane).
Windows are NOT always plain glass panes — colored panes carry meaning
(white = domestic, red = chapel, yellow = warm domestic).

**P15 — Decorative stair-block trim ornament at gable peak.**
*Seen on:* r00, r02 (crossed X-pattern at peak).
Pure-ornament stair-block clusters at the apex of gables. No structural
purpose; pure decoration.

### Tower / vertical patterns

**P16 — Twin towers flanking entry.**
*Seen on:* r10, r12.
Gate/entry is framed by two identical (or near-identical) towers on either
side. Reads as fortified entry / gatehouse.

**P17 — Conical-roof watchtower with railed balcony.**
*Seen on:* r14L.
Tower with an open-air observation balcony (dark-oak fence railing) ringing
the top floor, with the conical roof above.

**P18 — Ruined tower variant.**
*Seen on:* r14R.
Same tower but: uneven crenellations, broken-off chunks, missing roof
sections, leaning attached structures. Deliberate decay placement, not
random destruction.

**P19 — Stair tower / square turret rising from a domestic building.**
*Seen on:* r04, r15, r17, r22.
A single skinny stone tower (3×3 typically) rises above a domestic house,
topped with a conical or pyramid roof. Suggests defensive/important
function within an otherwise-vernacular building.

**P20 — Multi-storey vertical building with stacked gables.**
*Seen on:* r05 (massive — 5+ storeys), Abfielder tavern.
Multiple gables stacked vertically, each tier slightly inset, creating
extreme vertical emphasis. Reads as wealth, prominence.

### Decoration / atmosphere patterns

**P21 — Brick chimneys.**
*Universal in houses.* 1-block-wide stack of bricks or red blocks, often
with a chiseled cap (chiseled stone bricks), extending 4-8 blocks above
the roof line. Multiple chimneys on bigger houses.

**P22 — Plant overgrowth at edges and corners.**
*Seen on:* r04, r05, r07, r11, r17.
Vines, leaves, moss-carpet draped on wall edges, around windows, at corners.
NOT randomly scattered — deliberately placed where plants would actually grow
(north faces, damp corners, against shaded walls).

**P23 — Window-box flower planters.**
*Seen on:* r02, r07, r15, r17, r18.
Flower pots / sapling pots / colored terracotta beneath windows holding
flowers. Adds life and color punctuation.

**P24 — Exterior lighting at every door and corner.**
*Universal.* Wall torches, lanterns, or chains-with-lanterns at every door,
every porch, and every outside corner of a building. Lived-in feel.

## Micro-decorations (from r20 "30+ Medieval Decorations")

The decoration cheat-sheet image (r20) shows reusable micro-elements that
attach to or sit near larger builds. These deserve their own recipe folder
expansion later:

- Hanging signs from wooden brackets
- Wagon-wheel decorations (uses item-frame + wheel item, or stair circles)
- Hanging cured meat from ceiling beams (chains + items)
- Barrels with attached weapon racks
- Small magma forges (cube of stone bricks with magma core)
- Lanterns on multi-block chain drops
- Vine-covered fence posts
- Hayrick stacks for stables/courtyards
- Crate-and-barrel groupings beside doors
- Open shutters made from trapdoors

## The biggest corrections to my prior work

Looking at my dark_castle_v2 against these 22 references, the corrections
ranked by impact:

1. **My roofs are wrong.** Everything I build uses stepped pyramids. NO
   player build uses stepped pyramids for vernacular buildings. The fix
   is P1 (pitched gable for houses) and P2 (conical for towers).

2. **I don't decorate edges.** Every player build has plants, lights,
   flowers, signs at the corners and along the walls. My buildings are
   bare-walled blocks.

3. **I don't change material height-wise.** Player builds universally have
   stone bases and wood-or-half-timber tops (P10). My castles are
   stone-only top to bottom.

4. **My windows are holes with panes.** Player windows have frames, bay
   protrusions, diamond lattices, and stair-block hoods. I just punch
   pane-shaped holes in walls.

5. **My silhouettes are blocky-rectangular.** Player builds have multiple
   gable peaks at different heights (P20), stair towers rising from
   houses (P19), bay windows protruding (P11). My buildings are
   uniform-height boxes.

6. **My chimneys are missing or wrong.** Player builds always have multiple
   brick chimneys, prominently visible, with chiseled caps. I had one
   internal fireplace shaft and no external expression.

7. **My towers all look the same.** Player towers vary: intact vs ruined
   (P18), with vs without balcony (P17), domestic vs defensive vs fairytale.
   My four corners were identical.
